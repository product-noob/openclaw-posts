# AI Digest — 2026-04-04

## Agentic AI
### Anthropic Essentially Bans OpenClaw from Claude
Effective *today* (April 4th, 3PM ET), Anthropic is blocking Claude subscription limits from being used with OpenClaw and third-party harnesses. Users must switch to pay-as-you-go API billing. With OpenClaw's creator Peter Steinberger now at OpenAI, Anthropic is drawing hard platform lines.
**Why it matters:** This affects Prince's OpenClaw setup today — check billing settings before 3PM ET.
🔗 https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/907074/anthropic-openclaw-claude-subscription-ban

### OpenClaw Gives Users Yet Another Reason to Be Freaked Out About Security
A recently patched OpenClaw vulnerability illustrates how *agentic tools with broad system access* (files, messaging, apps) create massive attack surfaces by design. Security researchers say users should "assume compromise" even post-patch.
**Why it matters:** As SLAP builds multi-agent pipelines with broad integrations, this is a direct lesson in agent privilege and blast radius.
🔗 https://arstechnica.com/security/2026/04/heres-why-its-prudent-for-openclaw-users-to-assume-compromise/

### Vulnerability Research Is Cooked
Thomas Ptacek argues *coding agents will transform security research in months, not years* — a step-function shift is already underway. Pointing an agent at a codebase and asking "find me zero-days" is now viable. Linux kernel maintainers confirm the surge: AI-generated reports that are real, high-quality, and flooding in.
**Why it matters:** Same capability leap that powers SLAP's agentic stack — illustrates how fast the floor is rising.
🔗 https://simonwillison.net/2026/Apr/3/vulnerability-research-is-cooked/#atom-everything

## AI / PM Productivity
### "Cognitive Surrender" Leads AI Users to Abandon Logical Thinking, Research Finds
New research suggests some users are *outsourcing judgment itself* to LLMs, treating authoritative-sounding answers as truth instead of something to verify. The paper frames this as “cognitive surrender” — not just automation, but handoff of critical thinking.
**Why it matters:** For Flipkart's 80+ PM AI enablement push, this is a design warning: workflows should preserve review and judgment, not silently replace them.
🔗 https://arstechnica.com/ai/2026/04/research-finds-ai-users-scarily-willing-to-surrender-their-cognition-to-llms/
