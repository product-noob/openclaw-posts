## 🔍 Key Themes

- **Open models are becoming the default deployment lever**: Google’s Gemma 4 launched with Apache 2.0 licensing and multiple local-friendly sizes, while LangChain says open models like GLM-5 and MiniMax M2.7 are now near-frontier on file ops, tool use, and instruction following. For builders, the practical takeaway is that agent quality no longer requires closed models by default, so your constraints shift toward latency, memory, and integration quality rather than raw capability.

- **Inference and pricing are being split into more precise product tiers**: Google added Gemini API Flex and Priority tiers to separate cheap bursty workloads from predictable production serving, and OpenAI is moving Codex to pay-as-you-go for ChatGPT Business and Enterprise teams. Anthropic’s extra charges for OpenClaw and third-party tooling show the same pattern in reverse: vendors are monetizing not just tokens, but reliability, workflow shape, and external orchestration.

- **AI products now need native controls for safety, sandboxing, and policy enforcement**: Moonbounce raised $12M to turn moderation policies into consistent model behavior, Granola’s default link-sharing and training-use settings highlight how quickly product defaults become trust issues, and Simon Willison’s CSP-in-iframe testing shows builders actively hardening generated apps without separate domains. As agents and AI-native UIs get more powerful, the differentiator becomes whether you can ship controllable, isolated, and policy-aware systems rather than just impressive model demos.
