## Key Themes

- **AI is moving into the operating system layer**: Google’s offline iOS dictation app with Gemma models and ChatGPT’s integrations with DoorDash, Spotify, Uber, Canva, Figma, and Expedia both point to AI becoming a persistent interface, not just a chat box. Builders should design for agents that can work locally when possible and seamlessly invoke external tools when they need to complete real user workflows.

- **Distribution now depends on utility inside existing workflows**: The Alibaba Accio story shows small sellers using AI for product discovery and market analysis, while ChatGPT integrations make the assistant useful across commerce, travel, and creative apps. The winning product pattern is embedding AI where decisions happen—helping users pick what to build, buy, book, or create—rather than asking them to adopt a separate AI destination.

- **Infrastructure bets are scaling to match product ambition**: Anthropic’s “multiple gigawatts” expansion with Google and Broadcom underscores that next-gen assistants will be constrained by compute economics as much as model quality. For builders, this means latency, cost, and deployment mode—especially offline-first and hybrid-cloud inference—are now product features, not just backend concerns.
