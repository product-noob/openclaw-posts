## 🔍 Key Themes

- **From Chatbot to Workflow OS**: OpenAI’s new guidance on Projects and Skills shows ChatGPT moving from single-turn prompting to persistent, reusable workspaces and repeatable automation. For builders, the signal is to design for stateful context, shared artifacts, and packaged workflows so users can come back to the same system instead of rebuilding prompts every session.

- **Platform Control Is Now Part of Product Strategy**: OpenAI’s Axios compromise response—rotating macOS code-signing certificates and updating affected apps—shows that trust in AI developer tools depends on operational security, not just model quality. Anthropic’s temporary ban of OpenClaw’s creator from Claude after pricing changes hit users underscores that API-dependent products live under platform policy risk, so builders need contingency plans for auth, billing, and model-provider dependency.

- **Reusable AI Experiences Win on Consistency, Not Just Capability**: OpenAI’s “skills” concept is a productized way to turn ad hoc prompting into standardized task execution with more consistent outputs across runs. Combined with Projects’ persistent context, the direction is clear for agentic and conversational products: encode repeatability into the UX so users can reliably execute recurring jobs with fewer prompt tweaks and less manual orchestration.

### What is the lethal trifecta?
**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rVBq12AgAJU | **Tags:** #YouTube #lenny_rachitsky
A builder-focused explanation of prompt injection risk in LLM applications, centered on the dangerous combination of untrusted input, access to sensitive data, and tool-use permissions. It translates an emerging security issue into a practical design concern for teams shipping agentic or action-taking AI systems.

**Key takeaways:**
- Prompt injection is not just a model issue; it becomes dangerous when an app lets the model read untrusted content, access sensitive data, and act through tools or APIs.
- The “lethal trifecta” refers to the risky combination of untrusted input, private data, and external side effects/actionable tools.
- Security-minded AI design should minimize the model’s access to secrets, isolate untrusted content, and tightly constrain what actions the model can take.
**Why it matters:** Secure agent design is becoming a core product requirement as LLMs gain access to real data and real-world actions.
