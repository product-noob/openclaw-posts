## 🔍 Key Themes

- **Regulated pilots are becoming a go-to distribution channel**: Anthropic’s Mythos model is being pushed into bank testing even after a Defense Department supply-chain risk designation, showing that policy friction does not necessarily block enterprise adoption. For builders, the lesson is to design for compliance-ready deployment paths early, because regulated sandbox pilots can become a practical wedge into high-value workflows.

- **Coding is the new front line for model differentiation**: The Verge’s “AI code wars” framing around OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic shows that software development remains the clearest battleground for product-led AI. Builders should expect coding assistants to keep absorbing model improvements first, so product strategy needs tight evals around latency, repo context, tool use, and end-to-end task completion rather than generic chatbot quality.

- **Model branding is starting to matter as much as capability**: Both stories point to named models, not just generic APIs—Anthropic’s Mythos in banks and the major labs’ coding tools in competition—signaling that enterprise buyers and developers are making increasingly product-specific choices. For AI-native products, this means your wrapper layer has to justify itself with workflow fit, integration depth, and trust properties, not just access to the latest frontier model.
